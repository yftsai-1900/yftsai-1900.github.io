Here I listed the projects I have done in university courses and high school programs.

Course Projects

2025 - Electrodynamics(II): Knots in Electromagnetism
- Starting from Magnetohydrodynamics, we discuss the conservation of magnetic flux and helicity, identifying the relavant topological properties as linking numbers and self-linking numbers. We also describe the topological properties in terms of Hopfions for the situations with the presence of electric fields. -
2023 - Quantum Technology and Its Applications
I chose to report Quantinuum's realization of non-abelian topological order on ion trapped quantum computer. The report includes the introduction of non-abelian anyons and the properties of their H2 computer.
2021 - Statistical Mechanics(II): Polymer Physics
This final report review the critical phenomena of ideal chains studied in the book ”Scaling Concepts in Polymer Physics” published in 1979, written by the 1991 Nobel Prize Winner Pierre-Gilles de Gennes.
2021 - Computational physics: 1D Transverse Field Ising Model using Tensor Network
Use cyntx package to write tensor network for 1D transverse field Ising model, and calculate the ground state energy and correlation functions.
2021 - History of physics: Second-Order Phase Transition
After reading the original papers of the fundamental developments of modern physics in early 20th century, we were asked to write a report for one earliest paper of a fundamental idea. The topic I chose was Landau's second-order phase transition.
2019 - Experiemental Physics: Quadrabot
- Based on the experience with motor control, I built a quadrabot with self-made structures (designed using Solidworks), Arduino and servo motors. The quadrabot can perform simple movements such as stand up and sit down. -
2017 - High School Graduation Project: Eddy-Current Braking and Motor Control
- Using homemade driver, Arduino and brushless DC motor, I built a motor control system with PI control and eddy- current braking. This project showed my ability to code, build hardware and debug. In that period, I was fascinated by Boston Dynamics and Hugh Herr's biomechatronic research. -
