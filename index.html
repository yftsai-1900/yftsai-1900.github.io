I am a graduate student in National Tsing Hua University, Taiwan(R.O.C.), interested in the condensed matter physics motivated by phenomenology.

Now I am focusing on the thermal transport phenomena in magnetic insulators, especially those potentially with exotic states like QSL. In general, I am interested in finding exotic finite-temperature phenomena in (quasi-)two-dimensional materials, where the Mermin-Wagner theorem prohibits spontaneous symmetry breaking for systems with short-range interactions.

I learned a lot from our group members. Some of the short notes here reflects my learning journey on quantum information, generalized symmetries, TQFT, tensor networks, and the mathematics as the prerequisites.

The 1900 in my email is the year when the Boltzmann constant entered the theory of black body radiation.
