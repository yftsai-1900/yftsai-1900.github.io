I like to make connections between concepts that seem different at the first glance. However, not all of the connections are correct. The simplest non-trivial problems along with revelant mathematical theorems serve as beacons, helping me navigate the possibility sea of "realistic condensed matter models". Here's the three research topics I'm interested in right now:

- stability of magnetic order and their relation to non-invertible symmetries
- low-energy effective theories of quasi-2D spin models and their realizations
- transport phenomenon in quantum materials/quantum simulators
- general topics on topological defects and domain walls

All these interests are inspired by the oscillation signals in the thermal conductivity in layered magnetic material $RuCl_3$.
